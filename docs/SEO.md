## Coconut Oil FAQs

**Q1: What is the difference between coconut oil and virgin coconut oil?**
A: Coconut oil is extracted from dried coconut meat (copra), while virgin coconut oil (VCO) is extracted from fresh coconut milk without heat or chemicals. VCO has a higher lauric acid content (50–53%), fresher aroma, and more polyphenols — making it the premium choice for health applications.

**Q2: What is cold pressed coconut oil?**
A: Cold pressed coconut oil is extracted mechanically at low temperatures (below 49°C) without chemical solvents or heat treatment. This preserves the oil's natural nutrients, aroma, and fatty acid profile better than heat-extracted or refined oil.

**Q3: Why does coconut oil become solid in winter?**
A: Coconut oil solidifies at temperatures below approximately 24°C due to its high saturated fat content. This is completely normal and does not indicate spoilage. Simply warm the container gently to re-liquify.

**Q4: Is coconut oil healthy for daily cooking?**
A: Yes. Kerala families have used coconut oil as their primary cooking oil for over a thousand years. Its medium-chain fatty acids (MCFAs) are metabolised differently from long-chain fats. Use in moderation as part of a balanced diet.

**Q5: What is the smoke point of coconut oil?**
A: Unrefined/virgin coconut oil has a smoke point of approximately 175–177°C. Refined coconut oil has a higher smoke point of around 200–232°C. For high-temperature deep frying, refined coconut oil or peanut oil is recommended.

**Q6: How should coconut oil be stored?**
A: Store coconut oil in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight in an airtight container. Refrigeration is not required but extends shelf life. In Kerala's climate, it will remain liquid most of the year.

**Q7: What is the shelf life of your coconut oil?**
A: Our pure coconut oil has a shelf life of 18–24 months from the manufacture date when stored correctly. VCO has a shelf life of 12–18 months. Always check the best-before date on your pack.

## 9.1 — Featured Snippet Answer Blocks

**Q: What is cold pressed coconut oil?**

> Cold pressed coconut oil is coconut oil extracted mechanically from dried copra (coconut meat) at low temperatures, without the use of chemical solvents, heat treatment, or bleaching. The cold pressing process — maintaining temperatures below 49°C — preserves the oil's natural fatty acids, antioxidants, and characteristic coconut aroma. Cold pressed coconut oil from Kerala is recognised as superior to commercially refined coconut oil because it retains its nutritional integrity and authentic flavour. It is produced in Thrissur and other Kerala districts by FSSAI-certified manufacturers.

**Q: What are the benefits of virgin coconut oil?**

> Virgin coconut oil (VCO) provides: (1) High lauric acid content (50–53%) with antimicrobial and antiviral properties; (2) Medium-chain triglycerides (MCTs) that are rapidly metabolised for energy; (3) Natural polyphenols with antioxidant activity; (4) Anti-inflammatory effects from its unique fatty acid composition; (5) Skin and hair nourishment through direct topical application; (6) Support for oral hygiene through the oil pulling practice. Kerala-manufactured VCO is extracted from fresh coconut milk without heat, ensuring the highest quality and potency available.

**Q: Why is Kerala coconut oil considered the best in India?**

> Kerala coconut oil is considered superior for several reasons: (1) Kerala's unique coastal and laterite soil conditions produce coconuts with naturally higher oil content and distinct lauric acid concentrations; (2) Kerala has over a thousand years of coconut oil production tradition with established cold pressing knowledge; (3) Kerala coconut oil manufacturers operate under FSSAI oversight with rigorous quality standards; (4) The state's two annual monsoons and Western Ghats biodiversity create ideal growing conditions. Thrissur district is a major coconut oil manufacturing hub in Kerala.
